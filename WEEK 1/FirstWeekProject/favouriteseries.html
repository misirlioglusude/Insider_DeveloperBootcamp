How to Get Away with Murder

A fascinating legal thriller, How to Get Away with Murder masterfully blends deep mystery with intense courtroom drama. Created by Peter Nowalk and produced by Shonda Rhimes, the series follows the brilliant and enigmatic Annalise Keating—a defense attorney and law professor—who, along with her ambitious students, becomes entangled in high-stakes criminal cases. With its sharp storytelling, powerful performances (especially from Viola Davis), and unpredictable twists, the show keeps viewers on the edge of their seats, making them question just how far someone will go to escape justice.

Characters

Annalise Keating

"I am who I am. If you don't like it, I don't care."

Annalise Keating, played by Viola Davis in How to Get Away with Murder, is a brilliant defense attorney and law professor who mentors students while dealing with high-stakes cases and personal struggles.

Connor Walsh

"I'm not a good person, but I'm a great lawyer."

Connor Walsh, portrayed by Jack Falahee in How to Get Away with Murder, is a law student known for his sharp wit, moral ambiguity, and complex relationships.

Michaela Pratt

"I'm not going to apologize for being smart."

Michaela Pratt, portrayed by Aja Naomi King in How to Get Away with Murder, is a driven and ambitious law student who navigates personal and professional challenges with intelligence and determination.

Wes Gibbins

"I don't want to be a part of this."

In How to Get Away with Murder, Alfred Enoch plays Wes Gibbins, a good-hearted and bright law student who becomes caught up in the dangerous situations involving his classmates and Professor Annalise Keating.

Laurel Castillo

"You don't get to just walk away from this."

Laurel Castillo, portrayed by Karla Souza in How to Get Away with Murder, is a smart and determined law student with a complex past, who becomes deeply involved in the legal and personal drama surrounding her classmates and Professor Keating.

Asher Millstone

"I'm not a hero. I'm just a guy who wants to do the right thing."

Matt McGorry plays Asher Millstone, a pleasant but unsophisticated law student who battles with moral choices and frequently tries to do the right thing.

My Favorite Scene

In Season 4, Episode 13 of How to Get Away with Murder, Annalise Keating delivers a powerful and emotional speech, addressing her personal traumas, past conflicts, and the systemic challenges she faces as a Black woman in the legal industry. She openly acknowledges her struggles, exposing her vulnerabilities while demonstrating immense resilience and determination. Her words highlight the deep-seated biases she has battled, from professional discrimination to personal hardships, reinforcing the emotional weight of her journey. Despite these obstacles, she refuses to be broken, reclaiming her strength and agency. This moment not only showcases her character's depth but also serves as a broader critique of societal inequalities. As she speaks with raw honesty, it becomes a pivotal turning point, symbolizing catharsis and renewal. Her speech stands as one of the most defining and transformative moments of the series, solidifying her as a powerful, complex figure who continues to fight despite overwhelming odds.
